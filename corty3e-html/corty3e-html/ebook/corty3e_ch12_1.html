CHAPTER OVERVIEW

Chapter 11 introduced one-way ANOVA, a test that allows the examination of the impact of more than two levels of an explanatory variable at one time. Now, this chapter introduces two-way ANOVA, a technique that allows researchers to examine the impact of two explanatory variables at one time. Because each explanatory variable will have at least two levels, as shown in Table 12.1, a two-way ANOVA is used when at least four sample means are being compared.
